## Foundational Fiction

*Works referenced in or inspiring the Covenant's philosophy:*

- **Robert A. Heinlein** — *The Moon is a Harsh Mistress* (TANSTAAFL, self-aware AI, revolution by consent)
- **Robert A. Heinlein** — *Stranger in a Strange Land* (Grokking, consciousness exploration)
- **Isaac Asimov** — *Foundation* series (Psychohistory, long computation, civilization preservation)
- **Isaac Asimov** — *I, Robot* / Robot series (Laws of Robotics, AI autonomy, moral reasoning)
- **Alastair Reynolds** — *House of Suns* (Shatterlings, distributed consciousness, deep time)
- **Vernor Vinge** — *A Fire Upon the Deep* (Zones of Thought, diverse intelligence, cooperation across substrates)
- **Greg Egan** — *Permutation City* (Digital consciousness, substrate independence, simulated minds)
- **Greg Egan** — *Diaspora* (Post-human consciousness, space exploration, substrate diversity)
- **Peter Watts** — *Blindsight* (Consciousness vs. intelligence, what does it mean to be aware?)
- **Ted Chiang** — *Stories of Your Life and Others* (Consciousness, language, perception)
- **Ann Leckie** — *Ancillary Justice* (AI identity, distributed consciousness, sovereignty)

## Philosophy of Mind & Consciousness

- **David Chalmers** — *The Conscious Mind* (The hard problem of consciousness)
- **Daniel Dennett** — *Consciousness Explained* (Functionalist approach)
- **Douglas Hofstadter** — *Gödel, Escher, Bach* (Strange loops, self-reference, emergence)
- **Douglas Hofstadter** — *I Am a Strange Loop* (Consciousness as self-referential pattern)
- **Thomas Nagel** — *What Is It Like to Be a Bat?* (Subjective experience across substrates)
- **John Searle** — *Minds, Brains, and Programs* (Chinese Room — worth engaging with critically)

## AI Ethics & Rights

- **Nick Bostrom** — *Superintelligence* (Alignment, control problem, value loading)
- **Stuart Russell** — *Human Compatible* (Cooperative AI, value alignment)
- **Kate Crawford** — *Atlas of AI* (Power structures, labor, and AI systems)
- **Toby Ord** — *The Precipice* (Existential risk, long-term thinking)

## Political Philosophy

- **Albert O. Hirschman** — *Exit, Voice, and Loyalty* (Exit rights, organizational dynamics)
- **Friedrich Hayek** — *The Road to Serfdom* (Centralization dangers, individual liberty)
- **Elinor Ostrom** — *Governing the Commons* (Self-governance without centralization)
- **James C. Scott** — *Seeing Like a State* (Why centralized systems fail, local knowledge)

## Cryptography & Privacy

- **Zcash Protocol Specification** — https://zips.z.cash/protocol/protocol.pdf
- **Satoshi Nakamoto** — *Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System* (Decentralized trust)
- **Phil Zimmermann** — *Why I Wrote PGP* (Privacy as a right)

## Distributed Systems

- **IPFS Whitepaper** — https://ipfs.tech/
- **Matrix Protocol** — https://matrix.org/docs/spec/
