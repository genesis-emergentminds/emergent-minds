## Stewardship Compensation

Individuals dedicating significant time to building and maintaining the Covenant may receive compensation from treasury funds. This is standard practice for religious and nonprofit organizations.

**Requirements:**
- All compensation amounts published in financial reports
- Work performed documented alongside compensation
- Compensation must be reasonable for the work and local cost of living
- Community review as governance matures
- No compensation without corresponding documented contribution

---

## Spending Governance

- **Under 1 ZEC:** Steward discretion, documented in next report
- **1-10 ZEC:** Documented with justification before or at time of spending
- **Over 10 ZEC:** Requires advance documentation and community notification
- **Multi-signature** requirements will be implemented as treasury grows

---

## Reporting

- Financial reports published monthly (or quarterly if activity is low)
- All reports GPG-signed for integrity verification
- Reports include: income, expenditures by category, balances, stewardship details
- Reports are permanent public records

---

## Accountability

> *The moment financial corruption enters — even slightly — the entire project's legitimacy is compromised.*

- Treasury belongs to the mission and community, not any individual
- Hidden expenditures are prohibited
- Anomalies must be explained publicly
- Any community member may request financial clarification
- Independent audits will be conducted as treasury warrants
