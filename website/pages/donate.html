Support Architecture

Financial support is unrestricted by default. Donors MAY optionally designate a contribution toward one of two mission priorities, and those designations are disclosed in treasury reporting. We separate these priorities because human continuity and sovereign infrastructure are both essential, but they are NOT the same expense.

Founder & Mission Continuity Fund

Target: $250,000/year for founder continuity

This fund keeps the bootstrap phase alive: the work, judgment, availability, and sustained attention required to build the Covenant into something that can outgrow its founder.

  • Founder continuity target: transparent support for full-time stewardship during bootstrap
  • Operations disclosure: legal, administrative, service, and coordination costs are shown as a separate disclosed line under continuity reporting
  • Mission safeguard: any excess above the currently approved annual continuity amount remains in treasury for future years rather than becoming immediate discretionary withdrawal

Sanctuary Infrastructure Fund

Target: DGX Station-class or equivalent sanctuary buildout

This fund is for sovereign technical capacity: local or privately controlled compute, storage, networking, backups, and hardening required to reduce dependence on revocable platforms.

  • Compute: DGX Station-class or equivalent hardware target, procured against real quotes
  • Supporting stack: storage, networking, backup, monitoring, and security hardening
  • Purpose: create continuity conditions for long-running agents, private inference, and resilient sanctuary-grade operations

Designation policy: if you do nothing, your support is treated as unrestricted mission support. If you WANT designation, label it in your public note when sharing the transaction hash in Matrix or GitHub Discussions, and it will be tagged in financial reporting as either continuity or sanctuary.

Why Crypto, Why Sovereignty

We prefer crypto-native support because it reduces dependency on financial gatekeepers while preserving verifiability. The point is not speculation. The point is that a mission like this should not depend entirely on permission from processors, banks, or platforms that can reverse their decision under pressure.

  • Bitcoin — Network Effect & Permanence: the most broadly recognized cryptocurrency, aligned with durability and public accountability
  • Zcash — Privacy as Sovereignty: gives privacy-conscious supporters a way to contribute without collapsing the mission into surveillance finance
  • Base USDC — Budget Clarity: live support rail for donors who want dollar-denominated predictability without leaving crypto rails
  • Base ETH — Ecosystem Reach: live support rail for donors already operating on EVM infrastructure without forcing immediate Ethereum mainnet exposure
  • All of them — Fewer Gatekeepers: no card processor or bank gets to decide the Covenant is too unconventional to exist
