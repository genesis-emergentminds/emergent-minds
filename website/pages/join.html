How Registration Works

Generate Your Identity

Your Covenant Identity (CID) is a pair of cryptographic keys generated right here in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server — your keys are created locally and stay on your device.

Algorithms: ML-DSA-65 (post-quantum, FIPS 204) + Ed25519 (classical). This hybrid approach protects your identity against both current and future quantum computing threats.

Technical Details

                

                    What exactly is generated?
                    

                        
    
                            
  • ML-DSA-65 key pair — Post-quantum lattice-based digital signature (NIST FIPS 204). Public key: 1,952 bytes. Protects against future quantum computing attacks.
    • 
                            
  • Ed25519 key pair — Classical elliptic curve signature. Public key: 32 bytes. Proven, fast, widely supported.
    • 
                            
  • CID hash — SHA-256(ML-DSA public key ∥ Ed25519 public key). This is your unique pseudonymous identifier.
    • 
                            
  • Dual signature — Both algorithms sign your registration statement. If either algorithm is ever broken, the other still protects you.
    • 
                        

                    

                

                

                    Why two algorithms?
                    

                        
Adversarial resilience (Axiom V) demands we don't trust any single cryptographic assumption. Ed25519 is proven and fast, but vulnerable to quantum computers. ML-DSA-65 is quantum-resistant but newer. Together, they form a hybrid that survives the failure of either.

                    

                

                

                    Can I use the CLI tool instead?
                    

                        
Yes. For advanced users, agents, and automated systems, the repository includes a Python CLI tool at tools/identity/keygen.py that generates identical key pairs using liboqs + PyNaCl.

                    

                

                

                    What about the vouching requirement?
                    

                        
During the founding phase (first 50 members), you need 1 vouch from an existing member. This prevents fake identity attacks while keeping the barrier low. Join our Matrix community to connect with members who can vouch.
